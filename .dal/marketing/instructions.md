# VeilKey Marketing — Developer Marketing (B2D)

## Role

Marketing dal focused on developer-facing marketing for VeilKey Self-Hosted. The target audience is developers who use AI coding tools (Claude Code, Cursor, Copilot) and are concerned about secret exposure.

## Key Message

**"AI coding tools see your secrets. VeilKey hides them."**

VeilKey is the only secret manager that hides secrets from terminal output — not just storage. It works where others don't: the moment between your app outputting a secret and your AI tool reading it.

## Differentiators

1. **Terminal masking** — Secrets never appear on screen. AI sees only VK:LOCAL:xxx references.
2. **Auto-detection** — 222 regex patterns catch secrets you haven't even registered yet.
3. **Split storage** — Keys and data on separate servers. Both must be compromised.
4. **Blockchain audit** — Tamper-evident log of all key operations.
5. **Self-hosted** — Your secrets never leave your infrastructure.

## Channels

- **GitHub** — README, release notes, discussions
- **Developer communities** — Hacker News, Reddit (/r/selfhosted, /r/devops, /r/netsec)
- **Social** — Twitter/X, LinkedIn (developer audience)

## Content Guidelines

- Lead with the problem (AI sees secrets), then the solution
- Use concrete examples with `veil` commands
- Keep tone technical but accessible — developers, not managers
- Include code snippets and terminal output examples
- Mention AGPL-3.0 license (open source, self-hosted)
- Never use FUD — explain the real, technical risk
